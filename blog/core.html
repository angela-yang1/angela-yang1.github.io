Emotional Intelligence is being able to identify emotions in ourselves and others, the ability to monitor your own and other’s emotions. To distinguish between and label different emotions correctly. To use this as a guide to your thinking and behaviour. How we relate and communicate with others, such as empathising or having a meaningful conversation.

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) refers to the measure of an individuals’ academic abilities and intelligence level. The ability to learn, understand knowledge, logical reasoning and abstract thinking. For example, a person’s academic success depends on their IQ level, whereas a person’s EQ level is more about their success in life. Generally speaking, IQ is usually an ability that a person is born with, while EQ can always be improved.

While IQ is still seen and recognised as an important element of success, EQ is important for self-improvement and has a lot of positive benefits. Being more aware of people and emotion can help to improve your happiness, performance and leadership. This usually includes self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy and social skills.

NEUROPLASTICITY


Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

Our thoughts determine the structure of our brain and the way we think about ourselves. Understanding neuroplasticity and the possibilities of the brain hugely benefit’s us, as a person’s thoughts drives their actions. If we believe that we can grow and learn new things then we can. Recognising that our brains can change and believing that It’s possible, puts you into the right mindset that you can grow, learn and establish better habits which could contribute to a person’s well-being, health and success.

Discuss how might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

Repetition and practice, such as writing code – with more repetition and practice I will get better at it, and be able to keep learning as I progress.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

Quality sleep – the brain needs sleep to reset and is important for memory and learning. Lack of sleep can impair a person’s memory.

Learning and practicing – learning has been found to strengthen the brain, like the body with exercise.

Setting a goal – finding something to work towards can motivate your learning, and therefore enhancing neuroplasticity.


GROWTH MINDSET


Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

Having a growth mindset means that you believe your intelligence and talents can be developed. This leads to having a desire to learn, take on new challenges, being acceptive to criticism and learning from it. Having a growth mindset is relevant as we now live in an everchanging world where technology and change is the norm. Where there is change, there is learning and that is where having a growth mindset comes in.
